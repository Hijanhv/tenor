Tenor turns one yield bearing asset into two tradable tokens.

| Term | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **SY** | The yield bearing underlying, for example a Blend pool token or a tokenized treasury. In the demo it is a mock token called TSY. |
| **PT** | Principal token. Redeems for exactly 1.00 of the asset at maturity. Bought below par, the discount is a locked fixed rate. |
| **YT** | Yield token. Collects all the yield the SY earns until maturity. A pure position on the interest rate. |
| **Invariant** | PT plus YT always recombine into the original asset. |

## The intuition

A yield bearing asset is really two things bundled together: the promise to get
your principal back, and the stream of yield it throws off along the way. Tenor
unbundles them.

- If you only want certainty, hold PT. You know exactly what it is worth at
  maturity, which is 1.00, so buying it below par locks a fixed return.
- If you want exposure to the rate, hold YT. Its value rises when yield rises and
  falls when yield falls.

Because principal plus yield always equals the original asset, the two halves can
be recombined at any time.
